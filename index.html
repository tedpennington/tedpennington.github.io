About Me

A natural-born tinkerer and problem solver, I am endlessly fascinated by the ways in which technology can enrich our lives, provide new approaches for the challenges we face, and connect us in the common human experience we all share.

I've spent the better part of my adult life working in various facets of the music industry, primarily as an audio engineer/producer, but also as a touring musician. The unique technical-creative amalgam inherent in audio production is in very many ways analogous to the similar attributes that are threaded throughout the world of software development. And, it's my love of that combination that has brought me to the field and kept me continuously intrigued. I enjoy finding new and creative ways to solve problems, and am a tenacious and passionate troubleshooter when roadblocks appear.

In my free time, I am active in the local ham radio community, serving local charity organizations by providing communications coordination/services for their events, and training in emergency-preparedness to provide backup communications and IT connectivity in disaster scenarios. I'm also a weather geek, and a trained Skywarn spotter for the National Weather Service.

Having lived in Nashville for the last 10 years, I've seen the city continue to evolve in some pretty exciting ways, and I'm excited to be living here during such an explosive period of growth. I'm currently seeking career opportunities in software development in the Nashville area, as well as always looking for new civic and community coding projects in which to become involved.
